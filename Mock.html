Mock


From left to right I watched the trees,
from left to right I watched the trees swing,
Gently gently they swung up and down,
I watched them swing to all directions
Till darkness came.

The cloud was heavy,
I could not see the tip of the mountain,
For it was covered in dew,
One could not see one coming afar,
We were blind to our very fronts.

It got silent,
The cool breeze stop,
We seeked rain
But it seemes the heavens has shot her gate,
We cried aloud,
But it seems our cries drew back laughter.

It started to rain,
We got filled with joy,
For it seemed our prayers had been answered,
Suddenly the rain stopped,
The sun chocked the soil of rain dropping above,
We ask”What sort of rain is this?”
A rain that comes with much promise,
And takes our crops as it flows away,
Takes our crops to foreign soil
And plants our seeds,
The sun left behind bites the very depth of our feets.

We till the land,
But the land in return tills us back,
We water the soil,
But the soil refuses to hold its take,
It drains us of all our reserves,
And lives us to thirst and wander.

Who is to blame we ask?
The rain says it’s the sun for been too harsh,
The sun says it’s the rain for falling carelessly,
Each battle to see the destruction of the other,
And they say to us “Define your path
Define your path for the future generation”.


Mocks shoots us like bullets from guns,
Mocks slashes our backs like cutlasses
Willed by the killers of men,
Mocks lashes us Stokes of Cain 
From the Masters to their dogs.
