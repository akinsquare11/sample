Equality

We have stood still in the jungle so long,
We have danced to the beats of the drums
And dunk ourselves at the beach by rivers bay.
We savour shells of hale
In pots not much so our scale,
We put our fluids in baskets
And in cure our caskets.

What is a jungle with barren trees?
Not much so a desert of dirt.
A verse land with mouse and vipers,
A forest with dears and snipers.

We danced like snakes,
Dancing to the beats of the charmers,
We danced straight right until 
We were caught.
They said we were one like nuts
Only to cut out and break us in two.

We are all trees in the jungle they say,
All rats at the same side of the jungle,
They promised to lead,
And now their commands we read.
Like shepherds leading their lambs to slaughter
They held our feets and hands
And took our voices.
They took from us all we had.
A cabinet of wild wolves
Sitting on the affairs of the jungle.

For so long we cried out
And buried our heads in sadness
For a saviour.
One to take us out of bondage
Heal our wounds and take our bandage.
We have cried with careless care,
In and out with fear,
When shall we hear 
The talk of fair in our jungle?
